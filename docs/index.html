Privacy Notice Statement

The Tanjay City Nutrition Council collects maternal supplement data solely for health-monitoring and reporting. Records are stored securely and processed in compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173). Access is restricted by role, and Barangay Nutrition Scholars may only view or edit records within their assigned barangay.

System Guidelines

Formulas & Classifications

1. Body Mass Index (BMI)

The system computes BMI automatically from the encoded weight and height:

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ [ height (m) ]²

Height is entered in centimeters and converted to meters (÷ 100). The result is rounded to one decimal place.

Example: 55 kg, 160 cm → 55 ÷ (1.60)² = 55 ÷ 2.56 = 21.5 → Normal.

2. Nutritional Status Classification (BMI)

Classification BMI Range
Severely UnderweightBelow 16.0
Underweight16.0 – 18.4
Normal18.5 – 24.9
Overweight25.0 – 29.9
Obese30.0 and above

3. High-Risk Flag

Records classified as Severely Underweight, Underweight, or Obese are counted under the High Risk card on the dashboard.

4. Reproductive Age Brackets

Bracket Age (years)
10 – 1414 years and below
15 – 1915 to 19 years
20 – 4920 years and above

These classifications are applied automatically by the system when weight and height are encoded. BMI cut-offs follow the standard adult (WHO) ranges.
